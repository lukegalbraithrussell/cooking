Buttermilk biscuits

This is a simplified recipe that is inspired from Adam Ragusea's biscuits. I don't remember which video I based the recipe on but both are good to watch for visual cues.

Supplies

  • 1 measuring cup
  • 1 tablespoon
  • 1 teaspoon
  • Mixing bowl (bonus points if it's cold from sitting in the freezer)
  • Baking Tray
  • Parchment Paper

Ingredients

  • 2 cup flour
  • 1 stick butter
  • 5 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 heaping tbsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 tsp sugar
  • 1 cup buttermilk, cold (obv you wouldn't use warm lol but keep it in fridge until last second)

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to like 460ish. (I keep increasing it 5 degrees each time I do this lol)
  2. mix dry ingredients (flour, cornstarch, baking powder, salt, sugar)
  3. Grate the frozen butter right inside the mixing bowl. Tap the box grater to make sure not too much is sticking to the sides (some definitely will; it's fine)
  4. Mix the butter with the flour mixture.
  5. Put in freezer for 15 minutes while oven preheats (Don't skip this!!! I did once and it was bad. We really don't want the butter to melt before it bakes)
  6. Barely mix in the buttermilk. It’s okay if there’s still some flour in the bowl because you can use that for the next step!
  7. Mold it into a big lump, flour the surface, and roll it out to an inch thick. Flour the top, fold it over on itself, and roll it out again. Repeat for a total of seven folds. I always lose count, though.
  8. Cut out biscuits, probably squares to minimize trimmings
  9. Put the biscuits on a parchment-lined baking sheet. They won't expand outward so you'll be fine as long as they're not touching.
  10. Brush the tops with melted butter with a pastry brush. I always forget to do this step too
  11. Bake until puffy and golden, about 15 minutes.
  12. Take off baking sheet immediately so bottoms don't burn

(Bad) Variants

  • use whole wheat flour if you want sad biscuits that aren't that good
  • Bake on racks if you want them to envelop the racks on both sides sorta like kebabs and be annoying to get off

Eggs Hemingway

This is a simplified version since I'm not using hollandaise sauce, instead using a flavored compound butter and relying on the poached egg yolk to be enough yolk.

Idea is from Chef John but I use homemade biscuits instead of English muffins.

Supplies

  • Pot
  • Timer
  • Mezzaluna (or anything sharp lol)
  • Slotted spoon

Ingredients

  • 1 egg
  • Pre-packaged smoked salmon
  • Biscuit (from my other recipe!)
  • Dill
  • Butter
  • Lemon zest
  • Pepper
  • Rice vinegar

Steps

  1. Chop up dill and zest
  2. Mix butter, dill, zest and pepper
  3. Bring pot of water to a simmer, add splash of rice vinegar
  4. Cook poached egg in simmering water for 5 minutes
  5. Pull egg out with slotted spoon
  6. Spread butter on biscuit, place salmon, then egg

Strawberry french toast

This is version 14 of my attempt to make french toast in a way that reminds me of Sassy Onion in Salem, Oregon.

Supplies

  • Mixing Bowl (helps if put in freezer beforehand)
  • Knife
  • 1-cup measuring cup
  • 2 baking sheets
  • A rolling pin (or your fists)
  • Oven-safe cooling rack
  • Whisk (I use an immersion blender whisk attachment)
  • Tongs
  • A pie pan (flat surface is good for soaking bread in batter, ty @altonbrown)
  • Paper towels to set the cracked egg shells on to prevent counter contamination

Ingredients

Toast

Honestly I eyeball everything except the buttermilk and eggs. That's the only ratio that needs to stay consistent

  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 swish of vanilla
  • 1 loaf of challah bread
  • ~1/4 stick of Butter
  • 1 tspn Salt
  • Crushed corn flakes

Whipped cream

  • 1/2 to 1 cup cream
  • Strawberries
  • Sugar if needed for strawberries, anywhere from a spoonful to even a 1/4 cup

Steps

French toast

  1. Day before: cut challah bread into 1 inch thick slices and leave out to stale
  2. Preheat oven to 350
  3. Place rack on baking pan, put in oven
  4. Crush cornflakes with rolling pin on other baking pan.
  5. Whisk together cream, eggs, vanilla, salt in pie pan
  6. Dip bread in mixture for 45sec each side, flip with tongs

  7. Dredge bread in crushed cornflakes

  8. Fry toast in medium low heat, 1 tablespoon of butter until golden brown on each side

  9. I also like to hit the edges like I'm searing a steak but that probably doesn't do anything
  10. Store in oven until ready (If it's a suuuuper long wait maybe turn off the oven)
    • I lowkey think these are better the next day when you reheat them in the toaster oven, the butter smells even better somehow

Whipped Cream

  1. Dice strawberries; I like slicing them so each one is a circle because they look like flowers
  2. Add sugar if the strawberries are too tart; the earlier you do this the more juices will be drawn out which will make the whipped cream more pink
  3. Put cream in (preferably cold) mixing bowl
  4. Mix for a few minutes until medium(?) peaks form
  5. Fold strawberries into whipped cream

Banana bread

Courtesy of my mother, original recipe from a Marcia Woehlbrandt, 1981

Supplies

  • 1 bundt pan or, 5 small loaf pans
  • a large mixing bowl
  • a whisk and biceps of steel, or a hand mixer
  • oven
  • 10 fingers or a wooden spoon
  • 1 tsp (I treat 1 1/4 tsp as a heaping tsp)
  • 1/2 cup measuring cup (I've never seen a 1 cup that will actually fit into the a jar, and you need a 1/2 cup anyway)
  • 1/3 cup measuring cup

Ingredients

  • 2/3 cup softened butter
  • 1 2/3 cup sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla (lol. I do a metric glug)
  • 3 eggs
  • 2/3 cup buttermilk
  • 3 ripe bananas
  • 2 1/2 cup flour
  • 1 1/4 tsp baking soda
  • 1 1/4 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 cup chopped walnuts
  • 1 cup chocolate chips

Steps

  1. preheat oven to 350 (325 convection)
  2. cream butter and sugar
  3. bash bananas up, with your hands or a wooden spoon I guess if you're not feeling primal
  4. add in vanilla, bananas, and 1/2 of the buttermilk, mix well
  5. add dry ingredients, blend well
  6. add eggs and rest of buttermilk, mix well
  7. add walnuts and chocolate chips, blend by hand
  8. butter and flour pans to prevent sticking
  9. pour batter into pans
  10. Bake:
    • bundt pan: 50 - 55 minutes
    • 5 small loaf pans: 40 - 45 minutes
  11. Freezes well
